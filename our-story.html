Who We Are

Our Story

Fifteen years across denominations. One question that would not go away: what did the apostles actually teach?

We are hungry for the truth, even if that places us among the minority. Even if it comes down to one. If there is anything more beloved to us than the Word of God, it is a speck in the eye that will not allow us to see.

After fifteen years following Christ across different denominations, listening to international theologians, teachers, and writers, researching Scripture through historical references, creeds, and primary sources, and analysing events both past and present, we arrived at a conclusion we did not go looking for.

As someone once said: truth is stranger than fiction.
Indeed it is.

The time is at hand. Let us turn our hearts to the truth and come out of that which Scripture calls Babylon.

◆
The Journey

Fifteen years across denominations.

We have sat in evangelical churches, charismatic services, reformed congregations, and independent fellowships. We have listened to sermons from pastors with millions of followers and from unknown teachers in small rooms. We have read the creeds, the confessions, the systematic theologies, and the apologetics.

What we found consistently was a gap. Between what the apostles wrote and what was being taught. Between what Scripture says and what tradition had settled on. Between the questions people were carrying and the answers the institution was equipped to give.

How capable is current teaching at answering the genuine, challenging questions of people who are actually searching? How much of it is based on Scripture? How much has merged with the way of the world, the demands of a media platform, or the economics of a large organisation?

These were not comfortable questions. They led to uncomfortable places. We did not choose to arrive at the conclusions below. We followed the evidence.

◆
What We Found

Progressive conclusions from Scripture.

The following convictions were formed through years of research, cross-referencing primary sources, and testing against the apostolic text. We hold them not as a creed to be defended but as conclusions to be examined. Test them yourself.

  1. One God, Eternal Father, Almighty, Most High. Not a Trinity of co-equal persons, but the God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob, the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ.
  2. Jesus Christ is the Son of God. Known as Lord, Messiah, King, Mighty God, High Priest, and the Second Adam. Distinct from the Father who sent Him.
  3. The earth is not a spinning globe. Scripture describes a circular earth founded upon pillars, enclosed by a firmament upon which the heavenly waters rest.
  4. The Tabernacle is circular, not rectangular. The design God gave Moses embeds the constant Pi (3.14). The shadow of the actual heavenly dwelling.
  5. We are in the Short Season, not the Church Age. The millennial reign of Yeshua on this earth ended around 250 years ago. Colonisation and world wars were among the numerous attempts made to destroy what once stood in glory all over the world. What history calls the Dark Ages is the disguise of the millennial age.
  6. Secular Israel is not the true Israel by geography, biology, or biblical explanation. Mainstream Christian support for the modern secular state is unfounded by Scripture.
  7. Current world events follow a script written through media, religion, politics, and education progressively from the 17th century onward.
  8. The 66-book canon is incomplete. Books referenced within Scripture itself have been systematically excluded. Their exclusion serves a purpose.
  9. Western missionary Christianity is not the apostolic faith. It has been shaped by colonisation, political agendas, the prosperity economy, and institutional interests.
◆
Why We Share This

Not to argue. To invite.

We share these conclusions because we know there are many individuals who have reached similar places privately and have not found others willing to say plainly what they have come to believe. Search the Scriptures for yourself. Test everything. Hold fast to what is true.

"The Bereans examined the Scriptures every day to see if what Paul said was true."

Acts 17:11

Come as you are.

If this resonates, reach out. We are ordinary people who found the living Word and could not look away.
