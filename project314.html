The Discovery

Pi hidden in the Exodus Tabernacle texts

In 2014, engineer Andrew Hoy made a remarkable discovery buried in the Exodus Tabernacle texts: the most accurate approximation of Pi (π ≈ 3.14) known to the ancient world — accurate to within 0.05% — encoded in Exodus 26 and 27.

This was not a marginal find. The implications reshape everything traditionally assumed about the shape of God's dwelling place. The Tabernacle courtyard was not rectangular. It was circular — a dome, a tent, a structure whose very geometry points to an intelligent divine designer.

Could an omnipresent God who cannot be contained to three-dimensional space be confined to dwell in a two-dimensional rectangular shape? Project 314 argues — on the basis of the Hebrew Bible texts alone — that the answer is no. The circle, and the mathematics of π, are written into the very fabric of God's house.

Andrew Hoy founded Project 314 to research, educate, and ultimately restore this lost vision of the Exodus Tabernacle — both mentally, in the minds of the believing community, and one day physically, as a built structure true to the Biblical specifications.

What Project 314 Does

Restoring God's House

Biblical Research

Deep exegesis of the Exodus Tabernacle texts in their original Hebrew, demonstrating that the courtyard curtain dimensions encode Pi and describe a circular, domed structure — not a rectangle.

Education & Publication

Andrew Hoy has authored The House of El Shaddai for English readers and is developing Exploring God's House, a Hebrew-language-based deep dive into the Tabernacle texts for serious students.

Physical Restoration

The long-term vision of Project 314 is to physically reconstruct the Exodus Tabernacle according to the Biblical specifications — a true-to-text, circular, domed structure, the first of its kind in the modern world.

Community Engagement

Project 314 challenges churches, teachers and scholars to re-examine longstanding assumptions about God's dwelling place — hosting webinars, publishing articles, and responding to critics with the text alone.

Model Building

Physical small-scale models of the circular Tabernacle are available through Project 314, allowing people of all ages to engage with the discovery hands-on and carry the vision into their communities.

Mathematics & Scripture

Project 314 is named after the π rediscovery. The project demonstrates that the Hebrew Bible is not superstition but a product of an intelligent designer — with the mathematics to prove it.
