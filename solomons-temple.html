Solomon's Temple

First Temple geometry read in context — before Babylon flattened the pattern.

I. Inherited Model II. Scripture III. Sulley & Ezekiel IV. Arithmetic V. Pattern VI. Sources

I doubted the shoe-box temples in my heart long before I wrote a word. The Creator curves everything He calls good. Why would His house in Jerusalem be the one exception?

Section I
Before the Text

The inherited shoebox

Every museum model of Solomon's Temple shows the same thing: a rectangular stone hall with a flat roof or shallow pitch, copied from post-exilic descriptions and Babylonian gate architecture. That image was not what elders mourned when the Second Temple foundation was laid (Ezra 3:12).

Mainstream assumption

60 × 20 × 30 cubits = rectangular hall. Influenced by Second Temple's squarer 60 × 60 footprint and Ishtar Gate proportions after Babylonian exile.

Contextual reading

Circular plan with central dome. Twenty cubits as diameter. Sixty cubits as perimeter or decagonal arc. Oracle cube (20³) as holy centre within curved house — shadow of Ezekiel's vision.

Solomon's Temple was completed c. 959 BC, built of hewn stone and cedar before Jerusalem fell to Babylon. The Second Temple, rebuilt after decades surrounded by brick monumentalism, measures differently in Scripture. We believe later generations projected Babylon's boxes backward onto Solomon's house.

Section II
Primary Sources

What the kings recorded

1 Kings 6:2–3 (KJV)
And the house which king Solomon built for the Lord, the length thereof was threescore cubits, and the breadth thereof twenty cubits, and the height thereof thirty cubits. And the porch before the temple of the house, twenty cubits was the length thereof, according to the breadth of the house; and ten cubits was the breadth thereof before the house.

Porch length equals house length (sixty). Porch breadth equals house breadth (twenty). In a circular reading, twenty cubits is the diameter and sixty describes the outer perimeter or decagonal shell — the same grammatical move that unlocks pi in the tabernacle (God of Pi).

1 Kings 6:20
The oracle within: twenty cubits long, broad, and high — a perfect cube of Most Holy space at the centre.
1 Kings 7:23
Molten sea: ten cubits brim to brim, round; thirty cubits compass. Diameter 10, circumference 30 — biblical pi at the temple threshold.
2 Chronicles 3:4
Porch height 120 cubits — vertical ascent toward heaven, not flat Babylonian gate profile.
Haggai 2:3
Who is left that saw this house in her first glory? How do you see it now? Is it not in your eyes as nothing?
Section III
Prophetic Architecture

Sulley and Ezekiel's circle

Henry Sulley's Temple of Ezekiel's Prophecy (1887) renders Ezekiel 40–48 as a great circular enclosure: four outer towers like the four living creatures, twenty-four inner places like the elders, central throne where sacrifice points to the Lamb. Thomas Newberry's earlier model (1883) placed a prominent dome over Solomon's house.

Neither Victorian scholar had modern "nonlinear" language. Both saw what tradition had flattened: circumference, dome, and throne above firmament — the same grammar from Ezekiel 1:22–28.

Section IV
Arithmetic

Working the pattern

Rectangular assumption (comparison)

Volume ≈ 60 × 20 × 30 = 36,000 cubic cubits. Footprint = 1,200 sq cubits.

Circular reading (diameter = 20 cubits)

Radius = 10 cubits. Floor area = π × 10² ≈ 314.16 sq cubits.

Molten sea: C ÷ d = 30 ÷ 10 = 3 (scriptural π approximation).

Oracle cube: 20³ = 8,000 cubic cubits of Most Holy space at centre.

First Temple vs Second Temple

Solomon: 60 × 20 × 30 — elongated, lower width-to-height ratio.

Second Temple trends 60 × 60 — squarer footprint after exile among Babylonian brick architecture.

Section V
Pattern Continuity

Shadow becoming substance

Ark → tabernacle decagon → Solomonic dome → heavenly Jerusalem. Each stage scales the same question: will we read God's measurements in context, or import boxes from the civilization that carried us into exile?
