Our Foundation
John 17:3

"Now this is eternal life: that they know you, the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom you have sent."

Built on the Living Word,
not on stone or wood

2:22 Church is a community rooted entirely in Jesus Christ and the apostolic teaching. We take our name and our calling from two passages in 2 Timothy, a charge to pass on truth faithfully and to pursue righteousness with all who call on the Lord.

We hold to One God Theology, Jesus as the Son of God, risen and exalted to the right hand of the Father as Saviour, Lord and King of the world. The Holy Spirit is the living power by which the Almighty moves and works in creation.

We do not believe that any building, structure or institution constructed by human hands is a church or a temple. Living people are the church of God. Where believers gather, in homes, parks, halls, or open fields, there is the assembly of the Lord.

Why the name 2:2|2?

The name carries two foundations in one. Read left to right, 2:2 points to 2 Timothy 2:2, a charge to pass on faithful teaching. Continue and you see 2:22, 2 Timothy 2:22, a call to pursue righteousness in holy community. The shared final digit is deliberate: the two callings are inseparable.

We are not built on programmes, buildings or institutions. We are built on these two apostolic charges, faithful in teaching, fervent in community. Everything else flows from here.

What We Believe

Core Convictions

One God

Read the opening paragraph of any apostolic epistle. The pattern never changes. Every apostle, in the very first lines of every letter they wrote, identifies God and Jesus Christ as two distinct persons: God the Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ His Son. This is not buried in a theological argument deep inside the letter. It is how they introduce themselves. It is the declared faith of those who walked with Jesus, received the Holy Spirit at Pentecost, and built the church. Count the epistles yourself.

Romans 1:7 — Paul

"Grace and peace to you from God our Father and from the Lord Jesus Christ."

1 Corinthians 1:3 — Paul

"Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."

Galatians 1:3 — Paul

"Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."

Ephesians 1:2 — Paul

"Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."

Philippians 1:2 — Paul

"Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."

2 Thessalonians 1:2 — Paul

"Grace and peace to you from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."

1 Timothy 1:2 — Paul to Timothy

"Grace, mercy and peace from God the Father and Christ Jesus our Lord."

Titus 1:4 — Paul to Titus

"Grace and peace from God the Father and Christ Jesus our Saviour."

Philemon 1:3 — Paul

"Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."

2 Peter 1:2 — Peter

"Grace and peace be yours in abundance through the knowledge of God and of Jesus our Lord."

2 John 1:3 — John

"Grace, mercy and peace from God the Father and from Jesus Christ, the Father's Son."

Revelation 1:1 — John

"The revelation from Jesus Christ, which God gave him to show his servants what must soon take place."

Not one apostle, in the opening of any epistle, writes of a triune God. Every single one writes of God the Father and Jesus Christ the Son as two. This is the consistent, unbroken, unrehearsed theology of the entire apostolic witness.

Baptism in Jesus Name

The formula of baptism in the apostolic church was never disputed among those who were there. Peter commanded it at Pentecost. Paul re-baptised those who had received only John's baptism. The record of Acts gives us at least three clear witnesses — Peter, Paul, and the household of Cornelius — all baptised in the name of Jesus Christ. This is not a minority tradition. It is the apostolic practice.

Acts 2:38 — Peter

"Repent and be baptised, every one of you, in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins."

Acts 10:48 — Peter again

"So he ordered that they be baptised in the name of Jesus Christ."

Acts 19:5 — Paul

"On hearing this, they were baptised in the name of the Lord Jesus."

Acts 8:16 — Samaria

"Because the Holy Spirit had not yet come on any of them; they had simply been baptised in the name of the Lord Jesus."

The Living Church

Community halls, expensive buildings, theatres and auditoriums were never at the heart of God. People were. The apostles met in homes, in the temple courts, by riversides, in upper rooms, in marketplaces, in prisons. What made it the church was never the address. It was the people gathered, the Spirit present, and the name of Jesus honoured among them.

Acts 2:46

"Every day they continued to meet together in the temple courts. They broke bread in their homes and ate together with glad and sincere hearts."

Romans 16:5 — Paul

"Greet also the church that meets at their house."

Colossians 4:15

"Give my greetings to the brothers and sisters at Laodicea, and to Nympha and the church in her house."

Matthew 18:20

"For where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them."

A Life of Service

People are not called to preach and build platforms. They are called to serve. God is not at the service of man — man is at the service of God. We are not called to ask God to bless our plans. We are called to adopt God's perspective, lay down our own, and go where He sends us. Greatness in the kingdom is measured in service given, not position held.

Mark 10:43-45

"Whoever wants to become great among you must be your servant, and whoever wants to be first must be slave of all. For even the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many."

Romans 12:1

"Offer your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to God — this is your true and proper worship."

1 Peter 4:10

"Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God's grace in its various forms."

Galatians 5:13

"Serve one another humbly in love."

Apostolic Teaching

The apostles are the foundation of the church. Jesus Christ is the cornerstone. Every doctrine that was later agreed upon by councils, established by creeds, enforced by empires, or passed down through institutional Christianity must be tested against what the apostles themselves actually taught and wrote. Where it does not align with apostolic Scripture, it is not the faith once delivered to the saints. It is tradition built on tradition. We hold to the apostles' teaching. Everything else must justify itself before that bar.

Ephesians 2:20

"Built on the foundation of the apostles and prophets, with Christ Jesus himself as the chief cornerstone."

Acts 2:42

"They devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and to fellowship, to the breaking of bread and to prayer."

Galatians 1:8

"But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach a gospel other than the one we preached to you, let them be under God's curse."

Jude 1:3

"Contend for the faith that was once for all entrusted to God's holy people."

Giving from the Heart

The ten percent tithe is not observed in the book of Acts. It is not taught in the epistles. What is observed is people giving as there was need, giving to support the ministry where necessary, giving to relieve the poor among them. The early church in Jerusalem sold land and possessions and laid the proceeds at the apostles' feet — not to fund a building project, but to ensure no one among them was in want. We cancel the mandate of obligatory giving and return to what the apostles modelled and taught.

Acts 4:34-35

"There were no needy persons among them. For from time to time those who owned land or houses sold them, brought the money and put it at the apostles' feet, and it was distributed to anyone who had need."

2 Corinthians 9:7

"Each of you should give what you have decided in your heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver."

1 John 3:17

"If anyone has material possessions and sees a brother or sister in need but has no pity on them, how can the love of God be in that person?"

Romans 15:26

"For Macedonia and Achaia were pleased to make a contribution for the poor among the Lord's people in Jerusalem."

Life Together

Where People Gather

The word "church" has been abused in our generation. Buildings are mortgaged. Costly equipment, instruments, lights and production gear are purchased in the name of God. Congregations are financially drained to fund institutions that, on paper, are owned by a ministry, a company, or a pastoral family — not the people of God. This is not the church of the New Testament. It never was.

God loves gatherings of His people. He always has. What He loves is not the address or the acoustics. It is the people: sharing, caring, edifying, uplifting, showing compassion. A lunch table qualifies. A garden qualifies. A seashore, a forest, a park, a backyard, a living room — wherever people are comfortable gathering with their families, in short groups and in large ones, that is where the church is.

Acts 2:46

"Every day they continued to meet together in the temple courts. They broke bread in their homes and ate together with glad and sincere hearts, praising God and enjoying the favour of all the people."

Daily. In homes. Around food. No building required.

Acts 16:13 — Paul at Philippi

"On the Sabbath we went outside the city gate to the river, where we expected to find a place of prayer. We sat down and began to speak to the women who had gathered there."

A riverside. No venue booking. No sound system.

Acts 20:20 — Paul to the Ephesian elders

"You know that I have not hesitated to preach anything that would be helpful to you but have taught you publicly and from house to house."

House to house. The apostolic method was personal, not institutional.

Acts 28:30-31 — Paul in Rome

"For two whole years Paul stayed there in his own rented house and welcomed all who came to see him. He proclaimed the kingdom of God and taught about the Lord Jesus Christ."

A rented house. The gospel went out from there to the capital of the world.

Hebrews 10:24-25

"And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds, not giving up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but encouraging one another."

The command is to gather. The form is not specified. The purpose is clear.

1 Corinthians 14:26

"When you come together, each of you has a hymn, or a word of instruction, a revelation, a tongue or an interpretation. Everything must be done so that the church may be built up."

Everyone contributes. Not a performance. Not a production. A gathering of equals.
