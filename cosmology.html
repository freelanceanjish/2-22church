Scripture and Creation

Biblical Cosmology

What does Scripture actually say about the nature of the heavens, the earth, the sun, moon and stars? Setting aside what we have been taught and allowing the text to speak for itself.

The Firmament Circle of the Earth The Fixed Earth Sun, Moon and Stars Waters Above Four Corners Source

The material on this page draws substantially from Rob Skiba's research series One Man's Quest for Truth, specifically Part 4: Allowing the Bible to Speak for Itself, published at testingtheglobe.com. All scripture quotations are from the KJV unless otherwise noted. We present this material as a serious invitation to re-examine what the inspired text actually says about creation.

Genesis 1 and Job

The Firmament

The opening chapter of Genesis does not describe a planet floating in empty space. It describes a structure. On the second day, God creates a raqia — a firmament — to divide the waters above from the waters below. The Hebrew word raqia comes from riqqua, meaning something beaten or hammered out, as a craftsman beats metal into a bowl or a mirror.

The oldest book of the Bible, Job, confirms this explicitly:

Job 37:18 (KJV)
Hast thou with him spread out the sky, which is strong, and as a molten looking glass?

A molten looking glass is not air. It is not the vacuum of space. It is a physical, solid structure — hard and reflective. The prophet Ezekiel, centuries later, sees this same structure in his vision:

Ezekiel 1:22, 26
And the likeness of the firmament upon the heads of the living creature was as the colour of the terrible crystal... And above the firmament that was over their heads was the likeness of a throne, as the appearance of a sapphire stone.

The throne of God, Scripture consistently tells us, sits upon the firmament. Isaiah confirms it:

Isaiah 66:1
Thus saith the Lord, The heaven is my throne, and the earth is my footstool.

The Hebrew scholar Robert J. Schadewald summarises the scriptural picture plainly: "The vault of heaven is a crucial concept. The word firmament appears in the King James version of the Old Testament 17 times, and in each case it is translated from the Hebrew word raqiya, which meant the visible vault of the sky. Elihu asks Job, 'Can you beat out the vault of the skies, as he does, hard as a mirror of cast metal?' This shows that the Hebrews considered the vault of heaven a solid, physical object."

Psalm 19:1 states: "The heavens declare the glory of God and the firmament sheweth his handiwork." The firmament is something God made with His hands. Scripture stretches it like a curtain and spreads it like a tent:

Psalm 104:2
Who coverest thyself with light as with a garment: who stretchest out the heavens like a curtain.
Isaiah 40:22
He... stretcheth out the heavens as a curtain, and spreadeth them out as a tent to dwell in.
Isaiah 44:24
I am the Lord... that stretcheth forth the heavens alone; that spreadeth abroad the earth by myself.
Amos 9:6 (NASB)
The One who builds His upper chambers in the heavens and has founded His vaulted dome over the earth.
testingtheglobe.com by Rob Skiba. One Man's Quest for Truth series.

Genesis is direct on what the firmament contains: "God set them in the firmament of heaven to give light upon the earth" (Genesis 1:17). The sun, moon and stars are placed inside the firmament. Not beyond it. Not in outer space on the other side of it. Inside it, as lamps hung in a tent.

Isaiah and Proverbs

The Circle of the Earth

Isaiah 40:22 is the verse most frequently cited by those who claim the Bible teaches a spherical earth: "It is he that sitteth upon the circle of the earth." The Hebrew word translated "circle" here is chug. But is chug the word for a sphere? Consider that Isaiah himself uses a completely different word when he wants to describe a ball:

Isaiah 22:18
He will surely violently turn and toss thee like a ball into a large country.
The Hebrew word here is dur, meaning a spherical object you can throw. Isaiah knew the difference between a circle and a sphere, and chose different words for each.

Chug means circle, compass, or vault. You cannot toss a chug. You can inscribe one, as Solomon writes:

Proverbs 8:27 (ESV)
When He established the heavens, I was there, when He inscribed a circle on the face of the deep.
Job 26:10 (NASB)
He has inscribed a circle on the surface of the waters at the boundary of light and darkness.

A circle inscribed on the face of the deep. A boundary between light and darkness. The same word chug appears in Job 22:14, where God walks "on the circle of the sky" (HCSB) or "in the circuit of heaven" (KJV). Schadewald writes: "By extension, chug can mean roundness, as in a rounded dome or vault. In both verses, the use of chug implies a physical object, on which one can sit and walk."

"How do you inscribe a circle on the face of something to get a ball? The circle is something cut into something else. An ice-skating rink is a circle. We skate on it, going round and round, but never up, over and around. It is not a sphere. It is an enclosure in the shape of a circle." — Rob Skiba
Ancient Hebrew conception of the universe. The circular earth under the dome, with waters above and below.
Ancient Egypt. The goddess Nut depicted as the canopy of heaven stretched over the earth, consistent with cultures across the ancient Near East.

Foundations and Pillars

The Fixed Earth

The earth we are told was established and does not move. This is not a marginal teaching scattered across one or two passages. It runs consistently from Job (the oldest book) through the Psalms, the prophets and into Zechariah:

1 Chronicles 16:30
Fear before him, all the earth: the world also shall be stable, that it be not moved.
Psalm 93:1
The Lord reigneth... wherewith he hath girded himself: the world also is stablished, that it cannot be moved.
Psalm 96:10
Say among the heathen that the Lord reigneth: the world also shall be established that it shall not be moved.
Psalm 104:5
Who laid the foundations of the earth, that it should not be removed for ever.
Zechariah 1:11
We have walked to and fro through the earth, and, behold, all the earth sitteth still, and is at rest.
1 Samuel 2:8
For the pillars of the earth are the Lord's, and he hath set the world upon them.

YHWH Himself speaks directly to Job from the whirlwind and describes the earth's foundation in terms that are architectural, not orbital:

Job 38:4, 6 (KJV)
Where wast thou when I laid the foundations of the earth?... Whereupon are the foundations thereof fastened? or who laid the corner stone thereof?

How does one fasten the foundations of a rotating globe spinning at 1,000 miles per hour while orbiting the sun at 67,000 miles per hour? Where do the corner stones and pillars go in that model? Rob Skiba asks the question plainly: "Tossing out all preconceived ideas of a spinning globe, just reading the text by itself, no one would ever think anything different from what it says."

These passages also resolve what would otherwise be a problem for the globe model: the sun standing still. Scripture records this miracle not once but several times, and it is the sun that stops, not the earth:

Joshua 10:13
And the sun stood still, and the moon stayed, until the people had avenged themselves upon their enemies. So the sun stood still in the midst of heaven, and hasted not to go down about a whole day.
Habakkuk 3:11
The sun and moon stood still in their habitation: at the light of thine arrows they went, and at the shining of thy glittering spear.
Isaiah 38:8
Behold, I will bring again the shadow of the degrees which is gone down in the sun dial of Ahaz ten degrees backward. So the sun returned ten degrees.

On a globe model, all three of these accounts require the earth itself to stop rotating and in Isaiah's case to briefly reverse rotation. In a geocentric model with a stationary earth and a moving sun inside the firmament, they require nothing more than what they say: the sun stood still, and the sun went backward.

Genesis 1:14-17

Sun, Moon and Stars

Genesis is explicit about where the lights are placed. The word in is used four times in four verses, and each time it is referring to the firmament:

Genesis 1:14, 15, 17 (KJV)
And God said, Let there be lights in the firmament of the heaven... And let them be for lights in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth... And God set them in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth.

The sun, moon and stars are not outside the firmament in a vacuum of space billions of light years away. According to the plain text, they are inside it, moving within the enclosed structure above the earth. The Psalms describe the sun's movement accordingly:

Psalm 19:4, 6
In them hath he set a tabernacle for the sun... His going forth is from the end of the heaven, and his circuit unto the ends of it: and there is nothing hid from the heat thereof.

Ecclesiastes states it plainly: "The sun also ariseth, and the sun goeth down, and hasteth to his place where he arose" (Ecclesiastes 1:5). The sun moves. It has a circuit. It has a place it returns to.

At the end of all things, according to both the prophets and Yeshua himself, the stars will fall to earth and the heavens will be rolled up like a scroll:

Isaiah 34:4
And all the host of heaven shall be dissolved, and the heavens shall be rolled together as a scroll: and all their host shall fall down.
Matthew 24:29
...the sun be darkened, and the moon shall not give her light, and the stars shall fall from heaven, and the powers of the heavens shall be shaken.
Revelation 6:13, 14
And the stars of heaven fell unto the earth... And the heaven departed as a scroll when it is rolled together.
Revelation 8:10
And there fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp, and it fell upon the third part of the rivers.

If the stars are suns trillions of miles away, each with solar systems going around them, how do they fall to earth like figs from a fig tree? Only in a model where the stars are close, small, and inside the firmament does this language become literally possible rather than purely symbolic.

Scripture also associates stars with the angels directly. Job 38:7 describes the morning stars singing together at creation, and the sons of God shouting for joy. Revelation 9:1 describes a star falling from heaven "and to him was given the key of the bottomless pit." Stars, in Scripture, are frequently personal, sentient beings.

Genesis 1 and Psalm 148

Waters Above the Firmament

Genesis 1:7 states that God divided the waters under the firmament from the waters above the firmament. Those waters above are still there. They did not simply evaporate at the Flood or cease to be mentioned. Psalm 148 calls them to praise:

Psalm 148:4
Praise him, ye heavens of heavens, and ye waters that be above the heavens.

Psalm 104 describes God laying the beams of his chambers in those very waters: "Who layeth the beams of his chambers in the waters" (Psalm 104:3). And at the Flood, it is not merely rain that falls. It is the windows of heaven being opened:

Genesis 7:11
...the same day were all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened.

Windows. Not clouds. The deep both below and above is broken open simultaneously. This is consistent with a sealed structure containing vast bodies of water on both sides of the firmament, not with a planet surrounded by the vacuum of space.

Ends and Corners

Four Corners, Four Winds

From Job to Revelation, Scripture repeatedly refers to the ends and corners of the earth. These phrases appear with remarkable consistency and cannot simply be dismissed as poetic:

Isaiah 11:12
He shall gather together the dispersed of Judah from the four corners of the earth.
Revelation 7:1
I saw four angels standing on the four corners of the earth, holding the four winds of the earth.
Revelation 20:8
He will go out to deceive the nations at the four corners of the earth.
Matthew 24:31
He shall send his angels... and they shall gather together his elect from the four winds, from one end of heaven to the other.

Rob Skiba observes: "If we accept that the authors of Scripture were directly inspired by the Holy Spirit of the Creator Himself, don't you think they would have used better terms to describe a spinning globe orbiting the sun if that were indeed the case? A simple reading of the Scriptures absolutely does not paint a picture of a globe. No child, unhindered by the interpretation and indoctrination of a scientist or theologian, would ever get a spinning globe out of any of the above."

What Scripture does paint consistently is an enclosed, circular, stationary earth under a solid dome, with waters above and below, the sun and moon moving inside the firmament, foundations and pillars holding the whole structure in place, and the throne of the Almighty seated atop it all.
