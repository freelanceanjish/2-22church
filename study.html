Study Hub

Scripture, Structure, and the Geometry of Creation

The same call that fell on King Josiah has fallen on sincere people again: find the Book, measure what Moses and John actually wrote, and present Scripture with its original intent.

These studies ask whether Noah's ark, Moses's tabernacle, Solomon's temple, Heavenly Jerusalem, and the earth itself were buried under tradition. We built this hub to measure Scripture again and promote research that lets the text speak on its own terms.

The call of King Josiah

2 Kings 22:8, 11, 13 (KJV)
And Hilkiah the high priest said unto Shaphan the scribe, I have found the book of the law in the house of the Lord. And it came to pass, when the king had heard the words of the book of the law, that he rent his clothes. Go ye, inquire of the Lord for me, and for the people, and for all Judah, concerning the words of this book that is found: for great is the wrath of the Lord that is kindled against us, because our fathers have not hearkened unto the words of this book, to do according unto all that which is written concerning us.

We believe that same summons is on the world now. Sincere people are going back to the Book, rediscovering what tradition buried, measuring again what was written before Babylon, Rome, and western missions renamed everything. Josiah did not invent the law. He found it, read it, and presented it to the people with its original intent. That is what this Study hub is for.

The geometric shapes in creation are not decoration. They are the motivation to research whether Scripture's major structures were designed in contextual reference to the text, not in the shoeboxes religion handed us. A sunflower spirals. A pinecone breathes. A Barnsley fern repeats one rule at every scale. Why would the ark, tabernacle, temple, and city alone be flat rectangles when the Maker never builds that way in what He calls good?

Holy geometry as motivation

We did not set out to redraw biblical buildings. We set out to follow Scripture. But every time tradition handed us a box, something in us resisted. Not rebellion. Recognition. We had seen the same grammar in a sunflower head, a pinecone scale, a Barnsley fern repeating its leaf at every scale.

Those are engineered forms with soul and purpose. Creation has intelligence in its structures. Every structure God commanded must carry that same intelligence. The studies below show the popular traditional diagram, demonstrate why it fails, then walk through what contextual geometry proposes when Scripture is allowed to speak on its own terms.
