Scripture, Structure, and the Geometry of Creation

Before you open any single study, read why we began this work at all.

I doubted the shoe-box temples in my heart long before I wrote a word. Walking past sunflowers, pinecones, and ferns, I kept seeing the same thing: the Creator wove intelligence into form. Why would His ark, tabernacle, temple, and city alone look like rectangles we never find in what He made?

My experience with God's creation

I am the author of 2-22church.com. I did not set out to redraw biblical buildings. I set out to follow Scripture. But every time tradition handed me a box, something in me resisted. Not rebellion. Recognition. I had seen the same grammar in a sunflower head, a pinecone scale, a fern uncurling.

Those are not decorations. They are engineered. A sunflower packs seeds in Fibonacci spirals. A pinecone opens with phyllotaxis. A fern fiddlehead grows in a logarithmic spiral. Creation has soul, purpose, and intelligence in its structures. If the Maker instils holy geometry into a flower, why would His ark be a sailing hull Genesis never describes?

Every structure God commanded — ark, tabernacle, temple, city — must also carry soul, purpose, and intelligence. The studies below each show the popular traditional diagram, demonstrate why it fails, then walk through what contextual geometry proposes.
