Study Hub

Scripture, Structure, and the Geometry of Creation

These studies grew out of questions I could not leave alone, each one beginning with the familiar picture and asking whether Scripture actually supports what we were handed in religion and ritual.

I carry a conviction about holy geometry and a compassion for people still lost inside religion, ritual, and mainstream Christianity, because I was there too, trusting the flannelgraph and the museum wall while something in my chest said the picture did not match the text.

What these studies are

Most of what you will find in this section is not original research from 2:22 Church. These pages exist because we support and promote serious work that other men and women have already done, work that lets Scripture speak without the assumptions religion handed us on a tray.

Biblical Cosmology promotes testingtheglobe.com and the scripture research of Rob Skiba. God of Pi promotes Project314.org and the tabernacle discovery of Andrew Hoy. Noah's Ark, Solomon's Temple, and Heavenly Jerusalem promote Renformation.com and the architectural research of Project Arc. We built these study pages to pass that work on to people who are still searching.

2:22 Church had already come to much of the same understanding before we found these researchers, but without the thorough study they have given their lives to. When we finally read their work, it was like hearing our own questions answered by someone who had walked the cubits mile by mile. That is why we promote them openly. They earned it.

I believe something like the days of King Josiah is happening again, a global movement of sincere people going back to the Book, rediscovering what tradition buried, measuring again what Moses and John actually wrote. In Josiah's day the lost Torah was found in the house of the Lord and the nation wept when they heard how far they had drifted. Project Arc speaks of that same grief in Ezra 3:12, elders who had seen Solomon's house weeping when the foundation of the Second Temple was laid. Something in the pattern had been forgotten. I think we are living in the hour when the pattern is being found again.

Why holy geometry still matters to me

I did not wake up one morning wanting to redraw Noah's boat. I was simply trying to read Genesis with a sincere heart, and every picture I had been given looked wrong before I could explain why, whether it was a ship with a bow, a temple like a stone shoebox, or a city like a cold cube dropping from heaven.

When I walked outside I would see a sunflower head turned toward the light, a pinecone on the path, and when I learned the Barnsley fern I saw the same pattern repeating at every scale from a few simple rules, and I am not a botanist but a man who reads his Bible and looks at the world God actually made, where what I kept seeing was not flat walls and sharp corners but curve, spiral, circumference, and breath, form that does something and carries purpose inside it.

That is what I mean when I say structures have soul, not that they are alive like a person but that the design is not random, because a pinecone opens and closes because its angles solve a problem and a Barnsley fern builds an entire leaf from the same rule repeated at every scale, and if the Maker does that in what He calls good then His ark should not be a hull Genesis never mentions, His tabernacle should not be a flat tent box when Exodus hides pi in the curtains, and His city at the end of Revelation should not look like a warehouse in the sky. The researchers below helped me see that conviction written large in Scripture. I am grateful to them.
