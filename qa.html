Common Questions

Questions & Answers

Honest questions deserve honest answers from Scripture. Everything here can be tested against the apostolic text.

These are questions we are asked most often. We have answered each one directly, with the relevant Scripture, and without softening what the text actually says. Click any question to read the answer.

God
Is there one God or three? +

There is one God: the Eternal Father, the Almighty, the Most High. Every apostolic epistle opens by distinguishing God the Father from the Lord Jesus Christ as two distinct persons. The God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob is not a Trinity of co-equal persons. He is One.

Hear, O Israel: the Lord our God, the Lord is one.Deuteronomy 6:4
Is Jesus God? +

Jesus Christ is the Son of God. He is Lord, Messiah, King, High Priest, and the Second Adam. He is not the Eternal Father. The Father sent Him, exalted Him, and made Him Lord and Christ at His resurrection.

God has made this Jesus, whom you crucified, both Lord and Christ.Acts 2:36

The Son declares the Father. He is the image of the invisible God, the Father's authorised agent through whom all things were made and through whom we are saved.

Why does the Bible sometimes call Jesus God? +

The title God (theos) is applied to Jesus in a limited number of passages and in each case carries the meaning of divine authority and office, not the assertion that He is the Eternal Father. The consistent testimony of every apostle is that God is the Father and Jesus is His Son. Titles like Mighty God in Isaiah 9:6 are governmental titles of office given by the Father to the Son.

Did God appear in the Old Testament? +

The Most High God communicated through authorised agents throughout the Old Testament: the Angel of the Lord, the glory cloud, the pillar of fire, and the Word. These were real encounters but not the Eternal Father in His unveiled essence. No man can see the Father and live (Exodus 33:20). What was seen was the agent carrying God's Name and authority.

Jesus Christ
Was Jesus pre-existent before His birth? +

In the purpose and foreknowledge of God, yes. The Lamb was slain from the foundation of the world in God's eternal plan. In actual personal existence, Jesus is the Son born of Mary, the Second Adam, fully human and fully appointed by the Father for His mission.

What does it mean that Jesus is Lord? +

The Lordship of Jesus is a title and authority conferred upon Him by the Father after His resurrection. It is not a statement that He is the Eternal Father. The Father exalted Him, gave Him the Name above every name, and seated Him at His right hand.

Therefore God also has highly exalted Him and given Him the name which is above every name.Philippians 2:9
Is Jesus currently reigning? +

Yes. He was raised from the dead, ascended to the right hand of the Father, and is presently reigning as Lord and King. He is our living High Priest, interceding before the Father. He will return.

Baptism
How should baptism be performed? +

Baptism is by full immersion in the name of Jesus Christ, for the forgiveness of sins. This is the command of Peter at Pentecost and the consistent practice recorded throughout the book of Acts. It is not a tradition. It is the documented apostolic standard.

Repent and be baptised, every one of you, in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins.Acts 2:38
Does baptism save? +

Baptism is the appointed act of obedience in which forgiveness is received and the covenant is entered. It is inseparable from repentance and faith. It is not a work that earns salvation but the moment in which the death, burial, and resurrection of Christ are applied to the believer.

Do you not know that all of us who have been baptised into Christ Jesus were baptised into His death?Romans 6:3
The Earth and Creation
Is the earth a globe? +

We do not believe so. The consistent cosmological testimony of Scripture describes a circular earth founded upon pillars, enclosed by a firmament upon which the heavenly waters rest. This is the plain reading of the text from Genesis to Job to Isaiah to Revelation, not metaphor.

He sits enthroned above the circle of the earth... He stretches out the heavens like a canopy.Isaiah 40:22
What is the firmament? +

The firmament is the solid expanse described in Genesis 1, which divides the waters above from the waters below. It is called the raqia in Hebrew, a word that means a beaten or hammered-out expanse. Job 37:18 speaks of the skies spread out like a molten mirror. The firmament is real, structural, and part of God's designed creation.

The Church
What is the church? +

The church is the living assembly of people who belong to Jesus Christ. It is not a building, a legal entity, or a denominational structure. Where believers gather in His name, the church is present. No address required.

For where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them.Matthew 18:20
Do you need to attend a building to be part of the church? +

No. The apostles met in homes, by rivers, in prison, in the open air. What made it the church was never the address. It was the people gathered, the Spirit present, and the name of Jesus honoured among them.

Is tithing required? +

Obligatory tithing is not taught in the New Testament epistles. What the apostles modelled was voluntary, generous giving in response to genuine need, without compulsion and without institutional machinery.

Each of you should give what you have decided in your heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver.2 Corinthians 9:7
The Current Age
Are we in the Church Age? +

We do not believe so. We believe the millennial reign of Yeshua with His saints occurred on this earth and ended around 250 years ago. The current age is what we call the Short Season, a period of concentrated deception and destruction before the return of the King.

What do you make of current world events? +

Current events follow a pattern that has been constructed progressively through media, religion, politics, and education from the 17th century onward. It has been predicted more accurately by historians and analysts than by the church. Scripture speaks to it plainly for those with eyes to see.

Is secular Israel the fulfilment of biblical prophecy? +

No. Secular Israel is not the true Israel by geography, biology, or biblical explanation. The promises of God are fulfilled in the Messiah and in those who are His through faith. Unconditional political support for the modern secular state of Israel by the mainstream Christian community is not grounded in apostolic Scripture.
