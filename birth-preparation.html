What To Do Now

Most likely phase: pre-labor / cervical preparation.

These signs can continue for hours or days. Because the first labor dilated quickly, stay observant once contractions become regular.
  • Rest between activity. Save energy for labor.
  • Eat light, nourishing meals and drink fluids.
  • Gentle walking, breathing, side-lying rest, and warm bath can help comfort.
  • Time contractions if they become regular.
  • Keep birth documents, reports, clothes, and transport plan ready.

Scar Thickness — What It Means

When To Contact Birth Team

  • Contractions become regular and stronger
  • Waters break or leak
  • Baby movements reduce
  • Bleeding is more than light spotting
  • Pain becomes constant rather than wave-like
